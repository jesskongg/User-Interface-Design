The Basics


TFSA stands for Tax-Free Savings Account. Think of a tax-free savings account (TFSA) as a basket you put things into.


You can pick what to put in that basket from a variety of financial instruments —          ,           and yes, actual savings accounts. And whatever gains you make from the investments in that basket are tax-free.

bonds

stocks



Why was the TFSA created?


The Canadian government introduced TFSAs in 2009 as a way to encourage people to save money for retirement. Since you paid tax on the money you put into your TFSA, you won’t have to pay anything when you take money out.



How does it benefit you?


A TFSA allows you to set money aside in eligible investments and watch those savings grow tax-free throughout your lifetime.


• Interest, dividends, and capital gains earned in a TFSA are tax-free for life.


• Your TFSA savings can be withdrawn from your account at any time, for any reason, and all withdrawals are tax-free.


• And if you want, you can put back the amount you withdraw into your TFSA. However, you have to do it the following year so it will not impact your contribution room.

Important


• When you open a TFSA you don’t have to pay tax on the money you put in. Example: If you invest $1,000 right now and it becomes $10,000 by the time you retire, that $9,000 you’ll have earned is tax-free.


• There’s a limit to how much you can put into a TFSA. Right now, you can contribute a maximum of $5,500 a year. You’re eligible to start as soon as you’re 18 years old. Any leftover amount from the maximum contribution is carried over to the next year (it adds accumulates!).


• You are allowed to quickly and easily take money out of the TFSA at any time. There is no penalty to withdraw money— and if you do, the amount is added to how much you can contribute the following year. Example, withdrawing $5,000 this year means that next year you’ll be eligible to contribute the normal $5,500—plus $5,000.

How to Open a TFSA?


You can open a TFSA by...


1. Contacting your financial institution (bank or credit union) and have a representative help you open a TFSA


2. You can visit your financial institution's website and apply to open an account yourself
